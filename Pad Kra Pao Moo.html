Pad Kra Pao Moo


Pad Kra Pao Moo, or Thai Basil Pork Stir-Fry, is a delicious and aromatic dish that's quick to prepare.
It's typically served over rice and topped with a fried egg.

Ingredients

1 lb ground pork 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
4 cloves garlic, minced
2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to spice preference)
1 cup holy basil leaves (or Thai basil if available)
2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 tablespoon oyster sauce
1 teaspoon fish sauce
1 teaspoon sugar
1/4 cup chicken broth or water
Fried eggs, for serving (optional)
Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions

Prepare Ingredients:

If you haven't already, mince the garlic, chop the Thai bird's eye chilies, and measure the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar.

Cook Pork:

Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and chopped Thai bird's eye chilies. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until aromatic.
Add ground pork to the wok and cook until it's no longer pink.

Season the Pork:

Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar to the pork. Stir to combine.

Add Basil and Broth:

Add holy basil leaves to the wok and stir-fry until they wilt. Pour chicken broth or water into the wok and stir well. Let it simmer for a minute or two.

Serve:

Remove the wok from heat. Serve Pad Kra Pao Moo over cooked jasmine rice.
Optionally, top each serving with a fried egg.
