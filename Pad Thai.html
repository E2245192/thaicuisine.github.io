Pad Thai


Pad Thai is a popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish that combines sweet, sour, salty, and umami flavors.

Ingredients

8 oz rice noodles (flat, medium-width) 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup tofu, pressed and cubed (or shrimp, chicken, or a combination)
2 eggs, lightly beaten
1 cup bean sprouts
1 cup sliced green onions
1/2 cup chopped peanuts
Lime wedges, for serving

For the Sauce:

3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
1 tablespoon tamarind paste
1 tablespoon rice vinegar
1 tablespoon sugar
1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)

Instructions

Prepare Noodles:

Cook rice noodles according to package instructions.
Rinse under cold water to stop cooking and set aside.

Make the Sauce:

In a small bowl, whisk together fish sauce (or soy sauce), tamarind paste, rice vinegar, sugar, and chili powder until well combined.
Adjust the balance of flavors to your liking.

Stir-Fry Tofu (or Protein):

Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
Add minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until aromatic.
Add cubed tofu or your choice of protein (shrimp, chicken) and stir-fry until cooked through.

Add Eggs:

Push the tofu and garlic to one side of the wok, pour the beaten eggs into the other side.
Scramble the eggs until just set, then mix them with the tofu.

Combine Noodles and Sauce:

Add the cooked rice noodles to the wok.
Pour the prepared sauce over the noodles and toss everything together until the noodles are evenly coated.

Add Vegetables:

Add bean sprouts and sliced green onions to the wok. Toss to combine and heat through.
